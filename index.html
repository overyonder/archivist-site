How NAS Historian avoids rework

01 / CACHE

Dense binary history

A compact cache records Vault identity and digest state, turning subsequent scans and matching passes into indexed lookups instead of repeated discovery.

02 / ZFS

Snapshot-aware change detection

On ZFS, Historian can compare snapshots to identify filesystem changes in one sweep and restrict deeper work to the affected data.

03 / STATX

Reuse identity before bytes

On other Linux filesystems, statx metadata and inode identity avoid hashing files and links whose recorded identity still matches.

04 / TRUST

Digests where the files live

When configured, Historian can consume fs-verity digests or persist its own digests in extended attributes, eliminating future content rehashes while that trusted state remains valid.
