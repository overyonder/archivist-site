The archivist’s duties

Three jobs, done properly.

Most frontends stop at a list of files. Archivist accepts the whole job of keeping a historical collection playable: catalogue it, prove it, and have it ready to run.

  1. Duty Nº 1

    Catalogue

    Every title is identified across the preservation catalogues — No-Intro, Redump, TOSEC, MAME and specialist lists — then reviewed selection rules choose the one best release to shelve. One game, one ROM.

    1,477 selection rules & overrides
  2. Duty Nº 2

    Verify

    Every file is hashed and checked against the preservation record, so you can see exactly what’s present, what’s missing and what’s ready to play — down to the last byte.

    222,242 files applied to the interface in 0.42 s
  3. Duty Nº 3

    Prepare

    Archivist detects the emulators already on your system and arrives with more than 1,800 pre-written launch plans, from cassette-era computers to arcade boards. The launch knowledge comes with the product.

    82 emulators · 1,800+ mappings

The collector’s charter

Buy it once. Keep it forever.

Archivist is sold the way a museum sells a book, not the way a landlord collects rent. The licence is perpetual, the software works offline, and the continuity pledge is published before anyone is asked to pay.
